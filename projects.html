NUtrition

This project was a website designed to make grocery shopping easier for Northeastern students by consolidating data about inventory and prices from grocery stores near campus. Since this type of data was unavailable online, my team wanted to crowdsource the data from Northeastern students.

To do so, the website included a barcode scanner that had two functions. First, people could point their camera at a food product and retrieve nutritional information about the food from the Open Food Facts API. Second, once the barcode was scanned, people could opt to add the food into the website's database, allowing other students to view information about the food. We would incentivize students to participate in this program by offering grocery store credits to students who added a certain amount of food products to the database.

My team also wanted to ensure that the website was accessible for people with food allergies or dietary restrictions. By scanning the barcode, people could view all the labels associated with a food (e.g. gluten-free, dairy-free, vegan, etc). Additionally, the website had a smart search algorithm that allowed users to search for the types of products they needed. This search algorithm could distinguish between whether students were searching for a brand (e.g, "Hood milk") or a type of food (e.g. "vegan milk").

Tech:

  • Next.js
  • Python
  • MongoDB

TerraNova

This project was a neural network that determined the optimal use of land for brownfield redevelopment. Brownfields are underutilized or abandoned tracts of land that are highly contaminated from industrial use. By suggesting a better use for the land, this project aimed to reduce environmental damage and revitalize the surrounding community.

The neural network uses features that consider social and environmental factors, as well as the structure of the building currently located on the land. The dataset consisted of historical land use data from Boston.

My team also started working on integrating a frontend to make the results more interpretable for users.

Tech:

  • Python
  • Figma
